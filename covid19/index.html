HR Interview

Imagine you are the HR and interviewing candidates. One candidate is quite confident and sure of himself and has a little less technical knowledge, while the other has no confidence and you can sense his nervousness from the moment he stepped in. Now it might be the case that the latter is quite brilliant in technical aspects but since he was not able to present himself or even speak, you (HR) did not get any chance to figure out his technical skills.

Which candidate will you hire?

Obviously, the first one. He won the round due to his personality and confidence. This happens to the best of us. The fear of giving the wrong answer, or stammering keeps many restricted from speaking at all. The nervousness gets to you and it so happens that even when you knew the answer to the question been asked, you were unable to muster up the courage and speak. You watch tons of YouTube videos, surf Google for answers on how to build up your self-confidence, but there are many thing you forget. And these are very important for the boost you need for the final day.

Before Interview

Before Interview

Dress up well

This might seem trivial to some but in reality your outfit makes you feel strong and poised, ready to impress. Formals are stylish in a way you can never imagine. They give a dapper look and wins you respect. Always wear a perfect formal attire and see your self-esteem rise high.

Mock interview

"Practice makes a man perfect". So, get a friend or any teacher and ask them to interview you in a proper setting. This will give you an idea of your presence of mind is and how fast and accurately you can answer a question.

Self Pep-talk

When you wait for your name to be called for the interview, you need to relax. The negative thoughts of 'What will happen?', 'What if I go blank?', 'What if the HR asks very tough questions?', etc. are sure going to haunt you. But instead you can focus on telling yourself that it is all going to be fine. It is just you and the HR, not a whole audience to laugh and mock if you give a wrong answer. So compell yourself to speak up if you know the answers. Tell yourself that you have prepared for the Interview and you will be fine as long as you don't let your nervousness visible. Take deep breaths before going in and simply face the challenge (HR) with a polite smile.

Going through the company's website

Suppose after all this, you ace the interview but finally you are asked about, say company revenue, and what if you had forgotten to surf the website. If you weren't nervous till then, you surely will be as soon as this question is asked. The company details and the job description is something like a question that you just have to read about and answer. Do not ever forget to read about the company and regret later.

Etiquettes during interview

"First impression is the LASTING impression". After entering the cabin where the HR is seated behind his desk, do not go and directly sit on the chair provided for you. Do stand until you are so advised. Once you sit, do not move too much, fold your legs, fold your hands, or keep your hands on the table. Give the documents asked and SIT STRAIGHT. As the HR looks toward you, MAINTAIN EYE CONTACT. Do not look hither and thither. Answer the question to the best of your knowledge. The HR is just a person interested in knowing you and nobody to criticize you. Breathe normally and speak normally like you speak to a colleague while explaining something. Once you are done, leave with a proper greeting.
